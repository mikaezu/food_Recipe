Pork Menudo

A Filipino classic dish.

Brief History

Pork Menudo sounds like Spanish but it is, unequivocally, a classic Filipino dish. In the long tradition of Philippine cuisine, Pork Menudo is one of the thick tomato sauce-based dishes that uses pork as its meat ingredient. Other classic Filipino dishes that are tomato sauce-based are Afritada which, by tradition, uses chicken as its meat ingredient; Mechado uses beef; and Kaldereta, originally, uses goat.

the Filipino style of Menudo originated during the time of Spanish colonization in the Philippines. Sautéing is a method of cooking introduced by the Spaniards during their colony and therefore, the pork meat is first sautéed in garlic and onion before stewing in tomato sauce. This, by the way, is the traditional culinary method in cooking this delicious Filipino dish.

Cooking Time

30 mins

Difficulty

Easy

Servings

4 people

How to Cook Pork Menudo

Ingredients

  • 250 grams pork kasim, cut into small cubes
  • 250 grams pork liempo, cut into small cubes
  • 250 grams pork liver, cut in small cubes
  • 1 pc carrot, medium sized, cut in cubes
  • 2 pcs potatoes, medium sized, cut in cubes
  • 1/4 cup cooking oil
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pc onion, minced
  • 1 (250 g) pack tomato sauce
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 2 pcs pork cubes
  • ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp raisins
  • 1 cup cubed red and green bell pepper

Cooking Steps

    Step 1

  • Let's begin by sautéing the onion and garlic in hot oil until lightly browned.

    • Step 2

  • Add the pork kasim and liempo next and fry until slightly brown in color before adding the liver. Cook for 2 minutes.

    • Step 3

  • Drop in the Knorr Pork Broth Cubes and give this a nice stir to dissolve completely before adding the tomato sauce, water, sugar and black pepper. Mix this well.

    • Step 4

  • PNow, gently place the potatoes and carrots in the pan. Continue to cook over low heat until meat and potatoes are cooked through and the sauce has thickened.

    • Step 5

  • Finally, add raisins and bell peppers and just allow to cook for 2 minutes longer. If you're adding Bok-Choi, add at this point. Here's a dish that will bring the entire family together. Pork Menudo, just like home, is always close to your heart.

BIG difference tip

Marinating the meats in Knorr Liquid Seasoning will add more flavor to your dish.
